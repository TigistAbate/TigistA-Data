### Description
Analysed a sales dataset by cleaning the data, summarising sales by county and product with a PivotTable, and categorising sales volumes using the Excel SWITCH function.
### Organisation

Retail business

### Why is this important?

Retail businesses use sales data to monitor performance, identify trends and support business decisions.

### Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
## What I Did

- Cleaned the sales data by removing hidden spaces and converting the **Sales Volume** column to a numerical data type.
- Created a PivotTable to summarise sales volume by county and product.
- Used the Excel `SWITCH` function to categorise sales volumes as **High**, **Medium**, or **Low**.
- Checked the results to ensure the PivotTable summary and sales categories were accurate.
- Used Excel tools to organise, summarise and analyse the data for reporting.

### Key Findings

- The PivotTable made it easy to compare sales volumes across different counties and product categories.
- Laptops and printers recorded the highest sales volumes in several counties, while smartphones had lower sales in some areas.
- The `SWITCH` function successfully categorised sales as High, Medium or Low, making it easier to identify products with strong or weak sales performance.
- Cleaning the data by removing hidden spaces and converting the Sales Volume column to a numerical format ensured the PivotTable calculations and categorisation were accurate.
